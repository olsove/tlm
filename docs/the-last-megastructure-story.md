# The Last Megastructure

There is no record of who built it.

No origin system.  
No construction logs.  
No surviving civilisation that claims it.

Only fragments—scattered across dead archives and corrupted signals—refer to something vast drifting beyond mapped space.

They called it many things:
- The Ark
- The Halo
- The Engine
- The Tomb

But all surviving references converge on one phrase:

> **The Last Megastructure**

---

## The Discovery

It does not orbit a star.

It does not follow known physics.

It drifts.

Slowly. Deliberately. As if guided by a purpose no longer understood.

From a distance, it appears broken:
- colossal segments fractured apart
- entire regions dark and inactive
- energy signatures fluctuating unpredictably

Yet it is not dead.

Deep within its structure, something still runs.

Systems awaken when approached.  
Signals respond.  
Doors open—sometimes.

And sometimes… they don’t.

---

## What You Control

You are not a conqueror.

You are not an emperor.

You are a **custodian of a system you do not understand**.

When you first connect to the Megastructure, only a handful of zones respond:
- a failing energy core
- a fractured control lattice
- dormant subsystems waiting for input

Everything else is:
- unstable
- inaccessible
- or actively resisting restoration

Every action you take changes the structure.

Every system you activate reveals something… and hides something else.

---

## Story Framework (Shared Canon)

The opening era of *The Last Megastructure* begins after a chain of silent failures across known colonies:
- navigation beacons desynchronise
- relay gates collapse without warning
- archival constellations lose positional coherence

In the middle of that breakdown, a repeating signal appears from beyond mapped space.

It is not language.  
It is not noise.  
It is a pattern that behaves like a handshake.

Multiple expeditions attempt contact. Most never report back.  
Your mission is not the first. It is simply the first that receives sustained access.

You are assigned under a narrow mandate:
- stabilise what can be stabilised
- recover operational knowledge
- prevent cascading failure
- avoid irreversible activation of unknown systems

No political bloc can fully claim ownership. No legal framework clearly applies.  
The Megastructure is treated as a custodial emergency, not a frontier to be conquered.

---

## Narrative Throughline

The narrative should unfold in layers rather than answers.

Early phase:
- restore local function
- map safe paths
- learn which systems react to presence, time, and sequence

Middle phase:
- confront conflicting interpretations of recovered fragments
- choose between short-term stability and long-term unknown risk
- witness subsystems that appear to predict, or rehearse, your actions

Late phase:
- realise that "repair" and "activation" are not always different actions
- infer that prior custodians may have failed for mutually incompatible reasons
- decide what continuity means when truth remains partial

At no point should the player receive a single definitive explanation of origin or purpose.

---

## Persistent Tensions

To preserve tone across all variants, each chapter should maintain at least one of these tensions:
- control versus comprehension
- preservation versus adaptation
- signal versus interpretation
- isolation versus coordination
- survival versus meaning

These tensions should be expressed through system behaviour, scarce context, and consequences that remain legible but never fully predictable.

---

## The Truth (or What Remains of It)

The Megastructure was not built for one purpose.

It was:
- a generator
- a network
- a vessel
- a weapon
- a sanctuary

Or perhaps all of these at once.

You will not be told what it is.

You will **infer it**:
- through recovered fragments
- through system behaviour
- through anomalies that should not exist

The deeper you go, the less certain the answers become.

# Final Note

All three versions share the same foundation:

- A vast, ancient system
- Partial control
- Incomplete knowledge

But each answers a different question:

- **Drift Protocol** → *What is this system doing?*
- **Entropy Rising** → *Can it be sustained?*
- **Broken Orbit** → *Where— or what— is it now?*

The truth of the Megastructure is not given.

It is uncovered.

And even then…

…it may not be what you expect.
